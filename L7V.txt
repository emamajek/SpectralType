   Bright, nearby example of a "blue" sdL7. While considered
   "subdwarf", from the detailed study by Gonzales21, it doesn't
   appear to be particularly metal-poor ([Fe/H]~-0.3) and its velocity
   is not particularly high (i.e. likely 'old' thin disk, maybe thick
   disk, but not halo). The nearest object in the Kirkpatrick21 20pc
   sample study for which they adopted L7.0 for both optical and NIR
   types, however the object is considered a subdwarf by multiple
   authors - hence "sdL7".  Primary of wide binary with a T7.5 ULAS
   J141623.94+134836.3 (considered T7.5p or sdT7.5). Gonzales20
   presented a detailed study of the "d/sdL7+T7.5P" binary, and
   mentions that the system was "discovered independently via a
   variety of methods by Burningham+(2010), Schmidt+(2010), and
   Bowler+(2010)" and that it was initially overlooked due to the
   "unusually blue NIR color (J-K=1.03+-0.03) (Schmidt+2010)."

   surprising for a 'subdwarf' is that the objects tangential motion

   U,V,W = -17.84,+5.81,-38.4 km/s.  I estimate the kinematic
