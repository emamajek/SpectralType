O8.5V

The Morgan65/Walborn71/Walborn90 standard HD 46149 appears to have
survived to modern day as consistent O8.5V standard in the O-star
literature (in e.g, Galactic O-Star Spectroscopic Survey; Sota11),
despite appearing as the MK78 O8V standard.  Garrison94 does not list
a O8.5V anchor standard, and MK73 does not list a O8.5V dagger
standard.

Morgan65 standard:  HD 46149 - O8.5V (but later O8V in MK78?)
Abt68 standard:     HD 46149 - O8.5  (no luminosity standard)
Walborn71 standard: HD 46149 - O8.5V (Plate II)
Walborn90 standard: HD 46149 - O8.5V
Sota11 standard:    HD 46149 - O8.5V
       		    HD 57236 - O8.5V
		    HD 14633 - O8.5V 

Morgan55 classfied one star as O8.5V: (BD-08_4617; V=9.4)

Conti71 classified three stars as O8.5V: HD 46966 (V=6.9), HD 46149
(V=7.6) and HD 52533 (V=7.7).

Walborn72 did not classify any O stars as O8.5V.

# Primary Standard

HD 46149 (= "HD 46149-1" ; Martins11)
  O8: JM53,Morgan55,Hiltner56,Hoag59
  O8V: MK78(stan),Keenan85
  O8Vn: Martins11 
  O8.5: Morgan65
  O8.5V: Walborn71,Conti71,Mahy09,Holgado20
 *O8.5V((f)): Walborn90,Massey95
  O9V: Feast59

  Good continuity in use as O8.5V standard from Walborn, Conti, GOSS,
  even thought it was O8V in MK78.  M91: V=7.599+-0.021,
  B-V=0.172+-0.012, U-B=-0.801+-0.013 =>QBV=> (B-V)o=-0.314,
  (U-B)o=-1.116, E(B-V)= 0.465.  Unfortunately equally split between
  O8V and O8.5V.  I follow Walborn and adopt "O8.5V((f))". This
  appears to be a binary as Martins11 lists HD 46149-1 and 46149-2,
  with classifications "O8Vn" and "O8.5-9V".  Mahy11 lists the
  components as "O8V" and "B0-1".  Fruscione94 lists logN_HI = 21.30
  (from Shull85).  Teffs:
  21360K(21224,21500;GaiaDR3/GSP_Phot/AENEAS;discrepent!),
  36900+-500K(Holgado20),
  37000+-2000K(Mahy11),
  37000K(Martins11).  => median Teff =
  37000K. logg=4.23+-0.08(Holgado20).
