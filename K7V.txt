K7V

Garrison94 does not list a K7V anchor standard, but MK73 lists
61 Cyg B as the K7V dagger standard. 

Given 61 Cyg B = GJ 820B's long heritage (JM53,MK73,Boeshaar76,Keenan88)
and recent use (Kirkpatrick91,GrayAtlas,Henry02) as the K7V standard,
it should probably be considered the best K7V standard.

JohnsonMorgan53 on updates since MKK43 for K2-M5. "The single subdivision between K5V and M0V in the Atlas [MKK43] has been denoted by K7V - instead of K6V - to indicate that it is about midway between classes K5V and M0V. A few standard M dwarfs have been reclassified with the fixed point that Barnard's star is set at M5V." JM53 lists two K7V standards 61 Cyg B and BD+56_1458 (GJ 394) and classify two other stars as non-standard K7Vs (HD 151288 (=GJ 638), HD 157881 (=GJ 673)). 

KeenanMcNeil76 states "Between K5 and M0 we now use K7 for stars of all luminosity classes."

Keenan84 does not list K7 as a full subtype like K5 and M0, and "other
possible subdivisions are utilized only for interpolation whenever we
think that we can classify accurately enough to justify their
use. Thus when we call a star G3 we mean only that it is closer to G2
than to G5."

Note that Hawley97 considers both 61 Cyg B and GJ 380 to be 'K5', so
perhaps many K5 stars in their survey could be ~K7V?

star, followed by GJ 380 (J1011+4927).

The colors of 61 Cyg B do not match well the median colors of the K7V
stars in Hipparcos, suggesting systematic differences in
classifications of these objects by different authors (probably mostly
Houk & collaborators, as they do not list any spectral standards
between K5V and M2V).
