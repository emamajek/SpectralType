Kirkpatrick05: "Currently, no optical spectra have been acquired that
fill the aforementioned gap between L8 and T2. Oddly, the
near-infrared spectral sequence (as discussed below) shows a continuum
of spectra throughout this L8–T2 range without any gap. Note also in
Figure 2 that there are fewer T-subtypes in the optical—only T2, T5,
T6, and T8—than there are in the near-infrared because the optical
spectrum-to-spectrum differences do not warrant a grid any finer than
this. The T2, T5, T6, and T8 names for the optical subtypes were
chosen to parallel the types of those same objects under the
near-infrared scheme."

"For T dwarfs, the two classification schemes in use are those of
Burgasser et al. (2002a) and Geballe et al. (2002). The first of these
employs anchor points on the sky, and the second employs a set of
idealized ratios. Fortunately, both schemes yield very similar results
and both groups are now combining their ef- forts to produce a single
scheme that follows the MK Process (Burgasser, priv. comm.). The T
dwarf anchor points for this collaborative scheme are listed in Table
2."

The L/T gap is near (J-K,M_J)~=(1,14.5)mag (Leone21).

The original T dwarf grid from Burgasser02 did not include a T0V
standard (started with T1V).  The first object classified T0 was SDSS
0423-04 in Geballe02. The 'unified' near-IR T dwarf system appeared in
Burgasser06: https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2006ApJ...637.1067B/

Best20 (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2010.15853.pdf) shows that there is a
significant gap in the (J-K)_MKO colors (between ~0.9 and ~1.4) among
T0-T3 dwarfs after the L/T transition, as there is rapid
spectral/atmosphere evolution as brown dwarfs cool at ~1300K.

The nearest T0s in Kirkpatrick21 are: PSO J319.3102-29.6682 (2MASS
J21171431-2940034) (plx=76.1+-3.5mas), SDSSp J042348.57-041403.5
(plx=67.86+-1.51mas; but L6.5+T2 binary), 2MASS J21265916+7617440
(plx=60.28+-0.84mas), PSO J103.0927+41.4601 (plx=57.6+-3.3mas), WISEP
J015010.86+382724.3(plx=44.6+-3.2mas).

In Best21, the "L/T gap" transition is apparent in between (J-K)_MKO
color 0.9-1.4 (Fig. 6 & 7) and occurs between approximately abs mag
M_J_MKO ~14 and ~15.2. On the red side of the gap the hotter late L
dwarfs are fainter in absolute magnitude M_J_MKO than the bluer early
T dwarfs.
