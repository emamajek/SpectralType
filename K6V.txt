K6V

GJ 529 appears to be the primary standard. 

K6V is often not considered a full subtype.  Keenan84 does not list K6
as a full subtype like K5 and M0, and "other possible subdivisions are
utilized only for interpolation whenever we think that we can classify
accurately enough to justify their use. Thus when we call a star G3 we
mean only that it is closer to G2 than to G5."  Joy47 used K6 as a
single subtype step between K5 and M0 (and no K7/K8/K9).  Kuiper42
used all ten subdivisions for type K, including K6.
