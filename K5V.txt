Garrison94 lists 61 Cyg A as the K5V "anchor" standard and MK73 lists 61
Cyg A as the K5V "dagger" standard. Keenan80 listed it as a rare one
of "several standard stars defining the zero point and scale for both
temperature types and luminosity classes for types G, K, and M,
joining (for dwarfs) only the Sun (G2V) and sig Dra (K0V). One issue
is that all of the K5V standard stars appear to be metal poor, which
may skew the properties inferred for ~solar composition. Among older
papers one tends to find some stars classified as "K5" which on the
modern system would be later (~K6-M0) - and they tend to be redder as
well. For example, the median properties of stars *classified* K5 in
SIMBAD (which will include many older classifications) will tend to be
redder than those typed in more recent surveys that allowed for K6,
K7, etc. So when deriving typical properties, I try to focus on modern
classifications (Keenan89 and later, e.g. from surveys by
Gray/Corbally, etc.), and use literature metallicities to interpolate
the properties for K5Vs of solar metallicity.

The "Wielen Dip" that appears as a shallow dip in histograms of
absolute magnitude appears near Mv=7 (Wielen74, 1974HiA.....3..395W)
or M_G=7 (Guo21, 2021A&A...655A..45G).  Main sequence stars near M_G=7
should correspond mostly to K5V or K6V stars. Guo21 attributes the dip
to where H- begins to become the dominant opacity in stellar
photospheres.

# Comments on Teff scale as function of log(g)

I find:

K5V:   <Teff> = 4500 K , <logg> = 4.44.  
K5III: <Teff> = 3930 K , <logg> = 1.6. 

Presumably stars that appear "K5" with intermediate logg will have
intermediate Teffs (to first approximation):

Teff(K5) = (3609 K) + 200.7*(logg)
