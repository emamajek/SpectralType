F1V

Garrison94 does not list an F1V anchor standard, and MK73 does list an F1V 
dagger standard.

37 UMa appears to be the best F1V standard available.

Houk/Michigan classifications: Only 3 stars were classified F1V in
Houk's Michigan Spectral Catalogues (HD 29875, 40136, 110379). Two
were classified F2V by Gray et al. (2006), which relied on MK
standards from MK78 and the Gray/Garrison surveys from the late 1980s.
Houk/Michigan F1V stars appear to map to subtype F2V on the modern MK
system.

# No Standard

Houk75 does not provide an F1V standard, and does not treat F1 as a full
type (she does list five "F1" HD stars, but they all have comments)

JM53, Morgan65, MK73, Houk75, Cowley76, MK78, Keenan85,
GrayGarrison89, Gray89, Keenan85, GrayNstars also do not list a F1V
standard. Skiff08 does not flag any stars as F1V MK standards.

GrayGarrison89 and Gray89 do not define a F1V standard, but does list 14 stars
that they classify as F1V (including 37 UMa).

# Primary Standard

HD 91480 = 37 UMa
 *F1V: Roman49,Morgan53,Jaschek64(all),Cowley69(stan),Cowley74(stan),GrayGarrison89
  F0: Cannon
  F2V: Levato78,Gray03
  A9Vs: Adams35

  GrayGarrison89 did not list a F1V standard, but they do classify 37
  UMa as F1V, in agreement with Cowley (although Gray03 later calls it
  F2V?). Morgan53 lists only one F1V star (37 UMa), but it was not
  technically a standard.  At least decent agreement among Roman,
  Morgan, Cowley, Gray, Garrison on "F1V". V=5.16(HIP),
  B-V=0.349+-0.019(HIP), B-V=0.335+-0.005 (Mermilliod91),
  V-I=0.39+-0.03, 2MASS is saturated. Teffs: 6898+-80K(Casagrande11),
  6960(Gerbaldi07#2), 6972(Gray03), 6990(Gerbaldi07#1), Teff =
  7000(Suchkov03), 7003(Marsakov05), 7014K(Ammler-vonEiff12) =>
  <Teff>=6990 K. Only slightly subsolar - UMa member. Mass of ~1.5
  Msun and logg=4.3. logg=4.21 (Casagrande11). vsini=36.9+-4.0 km/s
  (Ammler-vonEiff12). 

# Weak Standard

HD 58946 = rho Gem 
  F0V:  Garcia89,Jaschek64,Jaschek78,Morgan65,JM53
  F0V+: Gray89 (MgII 4481A sl wk)
  F0:   Cannon 
  F1V:  Morgan71,Gray03
  F1mA9V: Garrison72(stan)
  F2-3VA S: Kennedy83 

  "A" is a spectroscopic binary (Worley96), so not an ideal standard.
  B-V = 0.316 (Mermilliod91).  No Houk type, literature types disagree
  a bit, and it is a spectroscopic binary. Morgan65 uses rho Gem as
  the F0V "fundamental classification standard", but then Morgan71
  calls its F1V. Too much use as F0V standard to take seriously as F1V
  standard.
