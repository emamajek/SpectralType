A8V

There appear to be no "classic" A8V standard stars, based on the
survey of spectral standards reflected in this file, Pecaut12 adopts
HD 158352 as an A8V standard.

Standards: 
Pecaut12:  HD 158352 - A8V 

Looking at the xHIP catalog (Hipparcos-based compilation by Anderson &
Francis 2012 with other ancillary data), one finds that among the A/F
dwarfs (lum class V) within 50pc from Gray01/Gray03/Gray06, there is a
well-defined gap between 0.26 < B-V < 0.29. There are reports of
various gaps amongst A/F (e.g.Bohm-Vitnse+70,D'Antona02). Remarkably,
the star on the blue side is none other than HR 8799
(B-V=0.259+-0.010, Mv=2.99; SpT=F0+VkA5mA5 (lambda Boo)) and the star
on the red side is HR 7848 = HIP 101612 (B-V=0.291+-0.012, Mv=2.53;
SpT=F0V).

# Standard

HD 158352 = HIP 85537 = HR 6507 
 *A8V: Cowley69,Abt95,Mora01(A8Vp),Pecaut12(stan)
  A5: Cannon

  Historically there were no A8V standard in the literature
  (e.g. papers and atlases by Morgan, Gray, Garrison, etc.).  Houk
  adopts A8V by cites "SIMBAD type listed; vsini=190 [km/s]."  Shell &
  Vega-type star. Based on the literature search shown here, Pecaut12
  adopts this star as the best available A8V standard. Pecaut12
  confirmed that the spectrum of HD 158352 is intermediate between A7V
  and F0V MK standards, and slightly hotter than the adopted A9V
  standard HD 73450 (BS Cnc). WDS lists a nonphysical companion
  (mag2=9.75) at sep=46.8". VSX liists it as a variable with amplitude
  0.01 mag and period 0.0756d - presumably delta Scuti? No evidence of
  unresolved binarity, but star is ~0.7 mag above MS (so I'm wondering
  if might be an ~equal mass binary?).  V=5.41(Haeggkvist66),
  V=5.418+-0.013(Mermilliod91), V=5.42(Hauck97), V=5.44(HIP),
  V=5.44(Cousins65) => adopt V=5.418.  B-V=0.215(Haeggkvist66),
