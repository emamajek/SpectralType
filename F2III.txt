  F5: Cannon

  Adopt "F2III (low vsini)" from GrayGarrison89. All published
  classifications since 1935 are "F2" subtype. Morgan flipflopped on
  luminosity class ("III" in MK43, "IV" in JM53, "III-IV" in MK73 and
  MK78). Gray has consistently called it "F2III" in four publications,
  and considers it the "F2III" standard, which I adopt.  V=2.28(HIP),
  B-V=0.380+-0.004(HIP), V-I=0.40+-0.03(HIP), plx=59.58+-0.38mas
  (HIP2), close enough we can assume negligible reddening.  Mv =
  1.16+-0.02 mag. Teff = 6792K(Erspamer03), 6810K(Prugniel07#1),
  6895K(Marsakov95), 6959K(Casagrande11), 7199K(Prugniel07#2) => adopt
  Teff=6900K.  logg=4.09(Prugniel07),
  logg=3.59(Erspamer03). [Fe/H]=-0.10(PRugniel07),
  -0.15(Erspamer03). Age: 1.0 Gyr (Marsakov95)

HR 7222 = HD 177392 = HIP 93603 (J1903+2116; V=6.5)
  *F2III: Harlan69,MorganAbt72(stan),Cowley76(standard),Penich81(metal weak),GrayNstars
   F1Vn: Gray01

   Adopt "F2III" from MorganAbt72. Using revised Hipparcos parallax
   (plx = 8.73+-0.48 mas), I estimate Mv=1.32, and the star is 1.36
   magnitudes above the main sequence. This would place it
   intermediate between the "III" and "IV" absolute magnitudes for F2.
   V=6.61(HIP), B-V=0.323+-0.004(HIP), V-I=0.37+-0.01(HIP),
   plx=8.73+-0.48 mas (HIP2). Reddening: E(b-y)=0.031 (Philip80),
   E(b-y)=0.03(Gray01). Adopting E(b-y)=0.03, then E(B-V)/E(b-y)=1.335
   from Lallemont14, then E(B-V)=0.04 mag. Hence (B-V)o=0.323-0.04 =
   0.283 mag. For R=3.33 appropriate for early F star, Av = 0.13
   mag. Including this extinction, then Mv = 1.19+-0.12
   mag. Teffs: 7050K (McDonald12).
