# COMMENTS ON COLORS OF A SUBGIANTS

I found 43 stars classified as A subgiants (IV) in the Hipparcos
catalog with distances < 75 pc and parallax S/N > 8 (probably mostly
Houk classiciations). Slash types and dashed luminosity classes were
not included. When the colors of subgiants were compared to average
dwarf colors for the same temperature subtype, I find an average
excess of:

<(B-V)AIV> - <(B-V)AV> = 0.027 +- 0.008 (stdev = 0.048 mag; N=43). 

Hence, for a given subtype, A subgiants are roughly 0.03 mag redder in
B-V than A dwarfs of the same temperature subtype.

Trends for individual subtypes:
<(B-V)A1IV> - <(B-V)A1V> =  0.019+-0.050 (stdev = 0.087 mag; N=3)
<(B-V)A2IV> - <(B-V)A2V> =  0.003+-0.020 (stdev = 0.054 mag; N=6)
<(B-V)A3IV> - <(B-V)A3V> =  0.046+-0.033 (stdev = 0.063 mag; N=5)
<(B-V)A4IV> - <(B-V)A4V> =  0.021+-0.024 (stdev = 0.036 mag; N=4)
