K4V

Garrison94 does not list a K4V anchor standard, and MK73 does not list a K4V dagger standard. Only two stars in the Bright Star Catalog (5th ed.) are classified as K4V (HR 5568 = HD 131977, HR 8721 = HD 216803). The primary (GJ 689 = HD 160964), secondary (GJ 879 = HD 216803 = TW PsA), and tertiary (GJ 570A = HD 131977) standards all have approximately solar [Fe/H] values, so the ~solar metallicity mean properties for K4V are well-defined.

Historically, 70 Oph B was listed as the dK4 dwarf spectral standard in the 1922 Report of the IAU Committee on Stellar Classification.Morgan65 classified two Hyads as K4V: HD 285720 & HD 285742, but they both seem to be systematically somewhat hotter and bluer than the other Keenan K4V standards. Pecaut16 finds that Houk/Michigan stars classified as "K3/4V" map well to "K4V" among the classifications of Gray03/06, and Houk/Michigan "K4V" maps roughly to "K4+V" on the Gray03/06 (note the Gray classifications use the Keenan standards).

The two brightest stars for which there seems to be strong consensus on K4V types are GJ 570A (HD 131977; V=5.71) and GJ 702B (70 Oph B; V=6.0).
