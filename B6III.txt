   Negueruela24 comments that all of the B6 stars in the Pleiades are
   evolved away from the main sequence, and comments that 17 Tau is a
   Be star ("although no emission lines are present in our spectrum").
   vsini=205km/s(David15). Earliest lum class III Pleiades
   standard. V=3.700+-0.009(Mermilliod91/Nobs>27),
   (B-V)=-0.113+-0.006(Mermilliod91/Nobs>30),
   U-B=-0.405+-0.006(Mermilliod91/Nobs>27). Q-method is calibrated
   using dwarfs, so may not be appropriate - I get (B-V)o=-0.115,
   (U-B)o=-0.407, E(B-V)=0.002, Av=0.007 - the reddening seems too low
   for Pleiads (typically E(B-V)~0.03-0.04).  Konyves07 reports far-IR
   loop/ring around Electra. plx=8.3457+-0.4386mas(GaiaDR3;
   RUWE=2.768).  Abt65 reports SB1 Porb=100.46d, e=0.52, K1=26.00km/s.
   Torres22: "The two astrometric companions discovered by lunar
   occultations may be one and the same (see Richichi et al. 1996). A
   100 day spectroscopic orbit reported by Abt et al. (1965) is
   spurious (see Torres 2020).  RV measurements were reported
   separately in the latter work."  E(B-V)=0.003(Naylor09),
   E(B-V)=0.030+-0.019(Paunzen24), E(B-V)=0.032(Kervella22/STILISM),
   E(B-V)=0.04(Friedemann92) => adopt E(B-V)=0.03 => Av=0.09. WDS
   lists two lunar occultation companions (OCC 424 Aa,Ab,Ac) with
   mag(Aa)=3.9, mag(Ab)=7.0(del(mag)=3.1), mag(Ac)=7.5(del(mag)=3.6),
   so they would be 5.8% and 3.6% as bright as Aa (combined
   delmag(Aa,Ab+Ac)=2.57) => using Mermilliod91 Vmag => V(Aa)=3.80,
   V(Ab+Ac)=6.37.  Teffs: 11588+-107K(Huson25), 12754+-504K(Fremat05),
   13150+-290K(Zorec16), 13308+-216K(David15),
   13484+-293K(Fremat05#2), 14655K(Chauville01),
   14700+-1000K(Gordon19; but assumes high E(B-V)=0.124) => adopt
   Teff=13396K. Gravity: logg=3.03+-0.25(Gordon19),
   logg=3.03(Chauville01), 3.412+-0.078(Fremat05),
   3.478+-0.113(David15).  Rad=5.91+-0.47Rsun(Gordon19).  Adopting
   mean distance for Pleiades (plx=7.367mas;GaiaDR3), Av=0.09, V=3.80
   => Mv=-1.95.
