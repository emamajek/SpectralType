Garrison94 lists HD 23585 as the F0V anchor type. MK73 does not
list an F0V dagger type.

The "modern" MK system of F-type dwarf standards is probably that
reflected in Table 1a of Gray89
(http://articles.adsabs.harvard.edu/full/1989AJ.....98.1049G).

The HD catalog (Cannon18;
http://articles.adsabs.harvard.edu/full/1918AnHar..91....1C) listed
"Typical stars" for "Class F0" as del Gem and alf Car. del Gem is now
classified as various variants (F2VkF0mF0 and F2V Fe-0.5 by Gray03,
A9V by Abt95, and F0V+ GrayGarrison89) and alf Car is now

Houk/Michigan classifications: I find that stars classified as "F0V"
by Houk in the Michigan Spectral Catalogues have median spectral type
"F1V" according to Gray et al. (2001, 2003, 2006) with rms scatter
~+-1.2 subtypes. This is likely due to Morgan changing the choice of
F0V standard in the 1970s (and modern wide-spread use of the MK78
types). Houk's survey started in the late 1960s and she adopted rho
Gem as a F0V standard following Johnson & Morgan (1953) - and Morgan
(1965) had reiterated use of this standard.  Unfortunately, MK78
switched the F0V standard to be HD 23585 - a somewhat hotter star than
rho Gem. Morgan (1971) and Gray et al. (2003) later classified rho Gem
to be F1V. Given the modern widespread use of the MK78, and
Gray/Garrison types based on MK78, if one wants to compare
Houk/Michigan types to modern classifications, I recommend converting
Houk's F0V stars to F1V.

HD 23585 appears to be a bit hotter (~7400K) and bluer (B-V ~ 0.256)
than the "mean" Teffs (~7100-7200K) and colors (B-V ~ 0.29-0.31) for
field F0V stars, however this is likely due to Morgan's switch of F0V
standard around 1970.

F0V and Teff ~ 7200K and B-V ~ 0.30 and Mv ~ 2.5 marks where the
"granulation boundary" crosses the main sequence (GrayNagel89;
1989ApJ...341..421G, Table 3). "On the cool side of the boundary, the
spectral line bisectors are of the classical type associated with
granulation. On the hote side the bisectors show a reversed slope and
curvature indicative of some other type of photospheric velocity
field, possibly having velocities substantially larger than typical
granulation velocities."

# Mapping of Houk/Michigan Spectral Survey Types to Modern MK Grid (Gray/Keenan) for F Dwarfs

See section 4.1 of Pecaut & Mamajek (2016) for a discussion on the subtle differences between specral classifications for dwarfs between the Michigan Spectral Survey catalog (Houk et al.) and those classified by Gray and collaborators on the modern MK grid (MK78 for hot stars through late F, Keenan standards for late F through M): https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2016MNRAS.461..794P/abstract). For the F dwarfs, PecautMamajek16 estimate the following mapping of types:

SpT(Houk) SpT(Gray)
F0V    => F1V
F1V    => F2V
F2V    => F3.5V
F2/3V  => F3.5V
F3V    => F4V
F3/5V  => F5V
F5V    => F5.5V
F5/6V  => F6V
F6V    => F6V
F6/7V  => F6.5V
F7V    => F7.5V
F7/8V  => F8V
F8V    => F8.5V
F8/G0V => F9V
G0V    => F9.5V
G0/1V  => F9.5V
